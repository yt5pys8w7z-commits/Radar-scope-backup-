Complete redesign of the airspace topology and traffic flow architecture.

Current problem:
the sector still feels like isolated random traffic moving through a graph network.

The sector must instead feel like:
- part of a larger European upper-area system
- connected to upstream and downstream sectors
- structured around organized traffic flows

1. Upstream / Downstream Logic
Routes must:
- originate from upstream sectors
- terminate into downstream sectors
- feel connected to larger traffic systems beyond the visible sector

Traffic should never feel:
- randomly spawned
- isolated
- disconnected from the network

2. Sector Group Structure
Create clear sector-group identities.

Example philosophy:
- North/West sectors:
  UK/oceanic overflight feed
- East sectors:
  Germany/Poland flow
- South sectors:
  Belgium/France/EHAM interaction

Traffic flows should visibly belong to different regional systems.

3. Major Directional Flows
Build several dominant traffic corridors:
- WEST → EAST overflight streams
- EAST → WEST counterflows
- NORTH ↕ SOUTH crossing flows

These flows should:
- remain mostly stable
- use structured airway-like corridors
- create recognizable traffic streams

4. PAM and SPY as Core Flow Nodes
PAM and SPY should become:
- central merge points
- crossing hotspots
- cognitive anchors
- tactical workload zones

Most important traffic interactions should naturally emerge around these areas.

5. Flight Level Allocation Structure (FLAS)
Add directional flight level structure logic.

Example philosophy:
- eastbound traffic prefers one FL band structure
- westbound traffic prefers opposite FL bands

Goal:
traffic direction should become visually readable through altitude organization.

6. Conditional Routes (CDRs)
Add simplified conditional route logic.

Requirements:
- some routes are only available when military airspace is inactive
- when military activity activates:
  - CDR routes close
  - traffic automatically reroutes through standard corridors

Goal:
create dynamic but understandable topology changes.

7. Route Philosophy
Avoid:
- spiderweb routing
- every fix connected to every fix
- random graph movement

Instead create:
- corridor-based flow systems
- merge points
- diverge points
- stable traffic streams
- predictable geometry

8. Cognitive Gameplay Goal
Player should start thinking:
- “this flow is becoming unstable”
- “PAM eastbound is building pressure”
- “south crossing will interfere with arrival stream”

NOT:
- “what is this random aircraft doing?”

9. Visual Goals
At a glance the player should recognize:
- dominant flows
- sector identities
- major crossings
- upstream/downstream traffic
- strategic hotspots

10. Important
- preserve readability
- preserve clean upper-area style
- avoid route over-density
- keep abstractions operational and believable
- prioritize traffic cognition over realism overload

Design target:
a living European-style upper-area traffic ecosystem
with structured directional flows, recognizable sector personalities,
and emergent operational pressure points.
